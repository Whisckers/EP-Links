A microwave link is a communications system that uses a beam of radio waves in the microwave frequency range to transmit video, audio, or data between two locations, which can be from just a few feet or meters to several miles or kilometers apart.

Modern Microwave Links can carry up to 400Mbps in a 56MHz channel using 256QAM modulation and IP header compression techniques.  Operating Distances for microwave links are determined by antenna size (gain), frequency band, and link capacity.  The availability of clear Line of Sight is crucial for Microwave links so that the Technology is able to communicate with each other.

Microwave links are commonly used by television broadcasters to transmit programmes across a country, for instance, or from an outside broadcast back to a studio. Mobile units can be camera mounted, allowing cameras the freedom to move around without trailing cables. These are often seen on the touchlines of sports ﬁelds on Steadicam systems.

Microwave signals are often divided into three categories:

  • ultra high frequency (UHF) (0.3-3 GHz)
  • super high frequency (SHF) (3-30 GHz)
  • extremely high frequency (EHF) (30-300 GHz)

A ber-optic cable is a type of cable that is used in computer networks to transmit data over long distances using light signals. It consists of one or more glass or plastic bers enclosed in a protective sheath. Fiber-optic cables are capable of carrying large amounts of data at extremely fast speeds, making them ideal for high-bandwidth applications such as internet connections, telecommunication networks, and data centers.

Advantages

  • Firstly, ber-optic cables have a much higher bandwidth capacity compared to traditional copper cables. This means they can carry more data at faster speeds.
  • Secondly, ber-optic cables are immune to electromagnetic interference, which can degrade the quality of signals in copper cables.
  • ber-optic cables have a much longer transmission distance compared to copper cables. While copper cables are limited to a few hundred meters, ber-optic cables can transmit data over several kilometers without any loss in signal quality.
  • ber-optic cables are also more secure compared to copper cables. Since they do not emit electromagnetic signals, it is dicult for hackers to tap into the data being transmitted through ber-optic cables.
